Louise de Broglie, Countess d'Haussonville by Jean Auguste Dominique Ingres

Fine Arts

When I was a teen I thought art was dumb. I never really understood what the hype around art was about. Often I would hear/see people "reading" paintings. About halfway through my first art history course in college, I experienced a light-bulb moment and felt as if I had experienced my first artistic sentence. The cover of this page, is indeed that painting. It is a famous neoclassical piece that uses the great physicist Louis de Broglie's aunt as the model. The artist, Ingres, was famous for his lofty neoclassical works that would celebrate high society. By using expensive material and texture such as the velvet of the dressing table and silky-reflective nature of the woman's dress Ingres showcases French high society, a style he was famous for.
