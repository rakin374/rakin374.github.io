To descibe a human, in a few paragraphs or less, is a difficult and errorprone task. Every human is involved in many parts of the the worldly affairs and exists as a mixture of archetypes. One human can take on the being of siblings, fathers, nurses, geniuses, and more. Sometimes simultaneously. In each of these stories/dramas, they are their own character. They may be heros in some, villians in others. They may be moral prophets in one and a criminal in the others.K Describing everything a person is into a short essay is probably the greatest data reduction problem. There is however good news in this. We humans can start new dramas, impact old ones, and engage in the choices of our life. This is my philosophy to human life.

An Important Question of Ethics: How does one become a good person? The defintion of good should contain properties such as moral, all caring, hateless. Being a good human is an important task for those of us who have giftful ability of self-reference. It is something that heavily concerns most of us humans. Learning to be a good person to those around us can be a difficult and painful experience. I don't mean because being good is hard, rather being bad is hard to witness through our own identity. It is my fundamental belief no human wants to cause harm to any other. There is an input of indeterminism through psychological states and other forms of luck, that instead pushes us towards it. Any how, this subject is filled with material of great thinkers that can be consulted. I have included it in this Hello page, to signify its importance to me.

a short bio

I was originally born in the densely populated city of Dhaka, Bangladesh. There life is hard. People eat with their hands. When I was about 6, I moved to the brisk land of the United States. I lived in the (puny but awesome) state of Connecticut. If you like pizza, you may have heard of a famous city located there. Connecticut has four seasons and people speak a clean dialect of English. For college I moved to the city of Boston. The city is like Vienna to me. There the favorite color is red. People are calm and friendly. Life is vibrant and different. The girls hurt a bit more. The drive from my part of Connecticut to Boston is 2 hours and 24 minutes. I wonder where else I'll go. Each location is filled with beautiful gifts. Each place I've been has provided me with great joy, humonously and posthumunously. Last updated 1124.
